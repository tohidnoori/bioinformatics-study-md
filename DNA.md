## What is DNA?  
DNA is the **genetic blueprint of life**. It contains instructions for making proteins and regulating cellular activities.  
- Located mainly in the **nucleus** of eukaryotic cells.  
- Also found in mitochondria and chloroplasts.

---

### Functions of DNA  
1. **Store genetic information**  
2. **Replication** before cell division  
3. **Code for proteins** (via transcription ‚Üí translation)  
4. **Transmit hereditary traits**  
### Structure of DNA  

#### 1. Nucleotides (Building Blocks)  
Each nucleotide has three parts:  
- **Phosphate group (PO‚ÇÑ¬≥‚Åª)**  
- **Deoxyribose sugar** (5-carbon sugar)  
- **Nitrogenous base**: Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Cytosine (C), Guanine (G)  

#### 2. Nitrogenous Bases  
- **Purines (2 rings):** Adenine (A), Guanine (G)  
- **Pyrimidines (1 ring):** Cytosine (C), Thymine (T)  

**Base-pairing rules (Chargaff‚Äôs Rule):**  
- A pairs with T (2 hydrogen bonds)  
- G pairs with C (3 hydrogen bonds)

## 3‚Ä≤ and 5‚Ä≤ Ends in DNA üß≠

### Basic Idea  
Each strand of DNA has a **direction** ‚Äî it runs from one end to the other.  
These ends are called the **5‚Ä≤ end** and the **3‚Ä≤ end**, based on the numbering of **carbon atoms** in the sugar (deoxyribose) of the DNA backbone.

### The Numbering of Carbons in Deoxyribose  
The sugar in DNA, **deoxyribose**, has five carbon atoms, numbered like this:

- **1‚Ä≤ carbon** ‚Üí attached to the nitrogenous base (A, T, G, or C).  
- **5‚Ä≤ carbon** ‚Üí attached to the **phosphate group**.  
- **3‚Ä≤ carbon** ‚Üí has a **hydroxyl group (‚ÄìOH)** that connects to the next nucleotide.

### DNA Strand Direction

Each DNA strand has a **directionality**:

- The **5‚Ä≤ end** ‚Üí has a **phosphate group** attached to the 5‚Ä≤ carbon.  
- The **3‚Ä≤ end** ‚Üí has a **hydroxyl group (‚ÄìOH)** on the 3‚Ä≤ carbon.  

DNA is always written and read in the **5‚Ä≤ ‚Üí 3‚Ä≤ direction**.


This arrangement is called **antiparallel**.  
It‚Äôs essential for base-pairing and DNA replication.

###  Why It Matters

1. **DNA Replication**  
   - DNA polymerase (the enzyme that builds DNA) can **only add new nucleotides to the 3‚Ä≤ end**.
   - So replication always occurs **5‚Ä≤ ‚Üí 3‚Ä≤**.

2. **Transcription**  
   - RNA is synthesized **from the 3‚Ä≤ ‚Üí 5‚Ä≤ DNA template**, making the RNA strand **5‚Ä≤ ‚Üí 3‚Ä≤**.

3. **Directionality = Functionality**  
   - Enzymes recognize strands by their 3‚Ä≤ and 5‚Ä≤ ends.
   - Mistakes in direction = no replication or incorrect transcription.
